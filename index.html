Addressing Mental Health on Campus

At Georgia Tech, students often face intense academic pressure, isolation, and burnout. While professional counseling services are available, many feel hesitant to seek help due to stigma or lack of awareness. Peer Support Circles aim to create accessible, informal spaces where students can share experiences, listen, and build community.

Our Poster

How It Works

Starting September 2025, we will host biweekly sessions in safe, informal locations such as West Village Lounge, Clough Commons, and the Student Center. Trained student leaders will guide conversations, ensuring everyone feels comfortable sharing only what they choose.

In addition, a monthly storytelling initiative will invite anonymous submissions of personal experiences. Selected stories will be featured on campus through posters, social media, and newsletters, helping normalize mental health discussions and fostering empathy.

Our goals:

  • Provide low-barrier entry points for students new to support groups
  • Combat isolation through community connections
  • Empower students with shared understanding
