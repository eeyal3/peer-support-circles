Addressing Mental Health on Campus

Georgia Tech students face intense academic pressure, isolation, and burnout. While counseling exists, many hesitate to seek help due to stigma or lack of awareness. Peer Support Circles create welcoming spaces where students can share, connect, and feel supported.

“I didn’t know how much I needed to talk until I joined. It’s like finding people who truly get it.” – GT Student

Our Poster

How It Works

Biweekly sessions start in September 2025 at West Village Lounge, Clough Commons, and the Student Center. Trained peer leaders guide discussions so everyone feels welcome and comfortable.

Monthly, anonymous stories will be shared across campus to help normalize mental health conversations and inspire empathy.

  • Provide low-barrier entry points for new participants
  • Combat isolation with community connections
  • Empower students with shared understanding
